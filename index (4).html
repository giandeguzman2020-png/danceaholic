Dance Styles

Dance styles are different ways or forms of dancing that have their own unique movements, techniques, and origins, each dance style expresses culture, emotion, and creativity in a special way. (Click the different images for a video clips)

1. HIP-HOP

Hip-hop dance is a lively and energetic street dance style that began in the 1970s.
It includes moves like breaking, popping, locking, and freestyle.
It’s all about rhythm, creativity, and expressing your own unique style.

2. WAACKING

Originating from the LGBTQ+ clubs of Los Angeles in the 1970s,
Waacking uses fast arm movements, dramatic poses, and expressive gestures.
It’s all about attitude, storytelling, and confidence — often danced to disco or pop music.

3. AFRO

Afro dance (also known as Afrobeats dance) is a lively, energetic dance style that originated from various African cultures, especially West Africa. It combines traditional African movements with modern music rhythms, often highlighting the hips, shoulders, and footwork.

4. HEELS

Heels dance is a modern dance style performed while wearing high heels. It combines movements from jazz, street dance, and hip-hop, focusing on confidence, control, balance, and expression.

5. KRUMPING

A high-energy street dance that expresses raw emotion and strength. Krumping involves powerful, exaggerated movements such as stomps, chest pops, and arm swings. It’s often used as a positive way to release emotion and energy.

6. VOUEGUING

Voguing is a bold and stylish dance that began in Harlem ballroom culture. It includes model-like poses, angular movements, and runway-inspired walks. Dancers express power, confidence, and individuality through movement.

7. TUTTING

Tutting focuses on creating geometric shapes with your arms and hands. Inspired by ancient Egyptian art, it uses sharp, clean angles and creative transitions. Often seen in hip-hop and popping routines.
